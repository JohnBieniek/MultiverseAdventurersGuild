How to Navigate the Rules

There are 3 levels to play.

Basic: Quick start and play. Perfect for one shots or introductions to the game. You start at Level "0". Read the text to build a character. Level "0" is about learning game concepts, most importantly the basic roll. The GM interprets and explains rules as you go. It's important to listen to the Heroes concept and translate it into game terms. Explain the tutorial nature of the level. The Hero doesn't need to know any rules to start with or even read anything. Incorporate Talents when the Heroes might need them.

Campaign: Using Talents demonstrates how to use them and how they affect gameplay. Talents are a core concept. At this point, encourage your Heroes to at least read the combat Talents. Ask your Heroes about the cool things they want to do and introduce them to the particular Talent.

Advanced Play: At this point, encourage your Heroes to read all the player material. Much of the material, in particular the section on Archetypes, will help advanced Heroes discover new character concepts. May consider starting at level 1 or higher.

Force, Energy, and Talents

Force (F): Maximum power tier you can use, unlocked by level.

Energy (EN): Resource you spend to activate Talents.

Slots: Number of sustained combat Talents you can keep active.

Force Energy Costs

Force 1 (F1): 1 energy sustained, 1 energy as a one-shot.

Force 2 (F2): 4 energy sustained, 2 energy as a one-shot.

Force 3 (F3): 9 energy sustained, 4 energy as a one-shot.

Force 4 (F4): 16 energy sustained, 8 energy as a one-shot.

One-shots last for one roll or one immediate use and never occupy a slot. Sustained effects do occupy a slot.

Tracking XP

Track two XP totals. Total XP earned determines a Hero's Level and never decreases when they buy an improvement. Unspent XP is the amount currently available to spend.

10 total XP earned = Level 1

20 total XP earned = Level 2

30 total XP earned = Level 3

Continue at 10 total XP per Level, up to Level 10.

Reaching a Level automatically grants everything listed for that Level, including HP, Energy, Talent Slots, Force access, Talents, Attack bonuses, or Defense bonuses. Heroes do not spend XP on those Level benefits, and spending unspent XP never delays Level progression.

Buying Improvements

Talents: Purchase any eligible Talent for 5 XP. A Hero cannot purchase a Talent above their currently unlocked Force Level.

Stats, Skills, and Attack Modifiers use the same escalating step costs:

+1 (Trained): 1 XP

+2 (Expert): 4 XP

+3 (Master): 9 XP

+4 (Legend): 16 XP

Each increase is purchased separately. Increasing a score from 0 to +3 costs 1 XP for +1, 4 XP for +2, and 9 XP for +3, for 14 XP total. Increasing a score from +2 to +3 costs only the next step: 9 XP.

Defense Rating has its own progression. Every Hero begins at +1 for free, giving Defense 11 from base 10. Later ratings must be purchased in order: +2 costs 2 XP, +3 costs 2 XP, +4 costs 4 XP, +5 costs 4 XP, +6 costs 8 XP, and +7 costs 8 XP. The cumulative costs are 0, 2, 4, 8, 12, 20, and 28 XP. Resilience and Ego cannot be purchased or upgraded; they are calculated only from their formulas.

Energy: XP can be spent to buy additional Energy, but the XP cost for additional Energy has not yet been defined.

Heroes may spend unspent XP whenever they choose, including during a mission. They may also switch their Talents whenever they reach a new Level.

Level

Level is based on total XP earned, not unspent XP. Every 10 total XP grants the next Level and its listed advantages.

HP = the base HP a Hero has at each level before Endurance is applied. Add the Hero's Endurance score once for each level they have, including level 0. Slots indicate how many combat Talents they can have active at any given time.

Force Access Rule

Heroes cannot activate or purchase Talents of a Force higher than their level allows.

0: Access Force 1 (F1) Talents, 1 Talent Slot, 10 HP

1: 2 Talents, choose 1: +1 Attack, +1 Defense, or +1 Talent; 16 HP, 3 Energy

2: Access Force 2 (F2) Talents, 22 HP, 6 Energy

3: +1 Talent, 28 HP, 9 Energy

4: +1 Talent Slot, choose 1: +1 Attack, +1 Defense, or +1 Talent; 34 HP, 12 Energy

5: Access Force 3 (F3) Talents, +1 Talent, 40 HP, 15 Energy

6: Choose 1: +1 Attack, +1 Defense, or +1 Talent; 46 HP, 18 Energy

7: +1 Talent Slot, +1 Talent, 52 HP, 21 Energy

8: Choose 1: +1 Attack, +1 Defense, or +1 Talent; 58 HP, 24 Energy

9: Access Force 4 (F4) Talents, +1 Talent, 64 HP, 27 Energy

10: 70 HP, 30 Energy, final Guild rank

Endurance increases are retroactive. If a Hero improves Endurance, apply the new bonus to all levels they already have.

Heroes can switch Talents when they reach a new level.
