Factions

It is illegal for Guild members to belong to a faction. Factions are secret networks, rogue philosophies, and hidden agendas inside or near the Guild. They are useful for recurring villains, compromised allies, suspicious mission sponsors, and long-running campaign mysteries.

Use factions as pressure, not homework. A faction should create strange requests, divided loyalties, suspicious evidence, and missions that feel useful at first but become more dangerous once the Heroes understand who benefits.

The Archivist Covenant (AC)

Motto: "The timeline remembers everything."

Secret Goal: The Archivist Covenant believes the multiverse's true timeline has been lost. They want to reconstruct the Primordial Canon by collecting data fragments from across realities.

What They Want: Prototype memory-extraction devices, quantum recorders, early AI logs, genetic samples from people who should not exist, and holographic or psychic event signatures.

Tells: AC agents over-document missions, keep secondary logs the Guild never asked for, and ask Heroes to retrieve items that seem trivial but oddly specific.

GM Uses: Heroes may collect fragments without realizing they are enabling a canon-reconstruction project. AC can oppose the Multiverse Preservation Society while still believing the Guild is too careless.

The Biocline Initiative (BI)

Motto: "Perfect lineage makes a perfect multiverse."

Secret Goal: Biocline tracks genetic drift across timelines. Its members believe the multiverse collapses when certain bloodlines diverge too far from their intended path.

What They Want: DNA from saviors, tyrants, inventors, mythic heroes, and timeline anomalies. A casual handshake, lost hair sample, or standard medical kit can become the real mission objective.

Tells: BI agents obsess over genealogy, baseline DNA, and anomalies. They carry medical technology above their station and request simple retrieval missions with suspiciously exact requirements.

GM Uses: Heroes may discover that their work lets Biocline clone, alter, or erase bloodlines in other universes. BI can become a villain faction or a morally ugly ally.

The Recursive Forgers (RF)

Motto: "History is written by those who write the revisions."

Secret Goal: Recursive Forgers manipulate documents, recordings, inscriptions, myths, and digital histories to edit reality through belief.

What They Want: Timeline-editing tools, memetic refractors, propaganda AIs, ancient tablets, prophetic scrolls, holy icons, and any artifact that shapes culture.

Tells: RF agents correct people's statements in conversation, replace mission notes with updated versions, and insist on retrieving the original copy of culturally important objects.

GM Uses: RF works well for social missions, stealth retrieval, forgery investigations, and campaigns where the Heroes realize that the evidence has changed since they last looked.

The Quantum Syndicate (QS)

Motto: "What if the best timeline is the one where we profit?"

Secret Goal: The Quantum Syndicate treats the multiverse as a marketplace. They trade weapons, spells, secrets, future knowledge, and contraband across timelines.

What They Want: Weapons before they are invented, spells before they are discovered, cybernetics before they are legal, future war outcomes, disaster forecasts, and supply requisitions they can use as smuggling cover.

Tells: QS agents never take missions unless there is a valuable junk item involved. They know too much about alternate futures and keep caches in dead timelines.

GM Uses: QS can act as recurring enemy merchants, dark-market quest givers, or tempting allies who pay well at the cost of timeline integrity.

The Echo Meridian Circle (EMC)

Motto: "The echoes of the future whisper truths the Guild will not hear."

Secret Goal: EMC believes the multiverse is dying from temporal acoustic collapse. They capture future echoes, prophecies, visions, and psychic shockwaves to prevent a cataclysm.

What They Want: Future-vision siphons, temporal resonance detectors, dreamcatcher circuitry, items destined to cause major events, and people with strong future-echo signatures.

Tells: EMC operatives speak cryptically about destiny, experience deja vu, know things before they happen, and request missions before the Guild officially issues them.

GM Uses: EMC can help or hinder Heroes depending on how they interpret a future echo. Their missions work well when stopping an event may be just as dangerous as allowing it.
