HP measures how much punishment your Hero can take before they are in serious danger.

Starting HP: 10 + your Endurance score.

Level HP: Heroes gain 6 + Endurance HP each time they gain a level after level 0.

Formula: Total HP = the base HP for your level + your Endurance score for each level you have, including level 0. For example, a level 3 Hero counts Endurance four times: level 0, level 1, level 2, and level 3.

Endurance increases are retroactive. If your Endurance improves later, apply that new bonus to every level you already have.

Example: A level 3 Hero with Endurance 0 has 28 HP. If that Hero raises Endurance to +1, the bonus applies across all four levels and their HP becomes 32.

Taking Damage

When an attack hits, subtract the damage from your current HP. Temporary HP is lost first. Normal healing cannot restore temporary HP unless a Talent specifically says it can.

At 0 HP

A Hero at 0 HP can only take a move action. They are still conscious enough to crawl, stagger, speak, or try to get out of danger, but they cannot fight normally.

Below 0 HP

After a Hero drops below 0 HP, no matter how far below, set them to -1 HP. They begin bleeding out.

Bleeding Out

At the start of each of their turns, a bleeding Hero loses 1 HP. At -5 HP, the Hero dies.

Stabilizing

Each turn, the Hero rolls to stabilize using Endurance as the modifier. The TN is 11. On a success, the Hero stops bleeding out.

A Hero can spend a Credit to automatically stabilize.

Healing

Healing in combat takes a move action and automatically stabilizes a Hero below 0 HP.

Outside combat, healing restores the maximum amount, but it takes 1 minute to complete.

Natural Recovery

After sleep, a Hero fully heals and regains all energy points.
