Reputation is what the Multiverse thinks it knows about you.

It might be true, exaggerated, outdated, or completely wrong. Pick a reputation that fits your concept, or choose one during play when the table discovers how people react to your Hero.

Anointed Champion - A temple, spirit, deity, prophecy, or movement has marked you as important.
Expertise: Helps with faithful communities, sacred sites, religious authority, and supernatural obligations.

Arcane Scholar - You are known for understanding old magic, impossible symbols, and dangerous rituals.
Expertise: Opens doors with mages, cult researchers, relic hunters, and anyone afraid of a curse.

Arena Favorite - Crowds know you from pit fights, tournaments, duels, or blood sport.
Expertise: Helps with fighters, gamblers, promoters, fans, and anyone impressed by public violence.

Blacklisted - Someone powerful has warned people not to work with you.
Expertise: Signals danger, reveals enemies, and opens doors with people who also hate that power.

Butcher - Whether fair or not, people believe you leave bodies behind.
Expertise: Frightens enemies, attracts violent employers, and makes peaceful NPCs wary or hostile.

Chaos Magnet - Trouble follows you, and everyone nearby knows it.
Expertise: Draws rumors, weird opportunities, reckless allies, and NPCs who want the impossible stirred up.

Clean Professional - You are known for doing jobs neatly, quietly, and on time.
Expertise: Earns trust from fixers, spies, nobles, and clients who value discretion.

Cold Operator - You are known for staying calm when everyone else breaks.
Expertise: Helps with hostage scenes, negotiations under threat, crisis command, and NPCs who respect discipline.

Dangerous Temper - People know you can be provoked into violence.
Expertise: Intimidates weak-willed enemies, scares off petty threats, and complicates delicate social scenes.

Dead-Eye Shot - People say you never miss when it matters.
Expertise: Gain attention from duelists, marksmen, bounty hunters, and anyone who needs a precision shooter.

Debt Collector - You are known for making people pay what they owe.
Expertise: Helps with intimidation, criminal negotiations, bounty work, and recovering stolen or promised goods.

Disgraced Officer - You once had rank, authority, or a badge, and lost it.
Expertise: Helps with police procedure, command habits, old colleagues, and understanding institutional rot.

Fixer's Favorite - Job brokers know you get results.
Expertise: Helps with paid work, introductions, better mission terms, and first notice of profitable opportunities.

Forbidden Student - You learned from a banned school, outlawed master, or sealed archive.
Expertise: Helps with forbidden lore, secret techniques, illegal texts, and NPCs who fear or covet that knowledge.

Former Corporate Asset - You used to belong to a company, agency, or power structure that invested heavily in you.
Expertise: Helps with corporate access, insider language, security procedures, and recognizing company tactics.

Friend of Spirits - Spirits, ghosts, animals, or unseen things seem willing to deal with you.
Expertise: Helps with omens, haunted places, nature spirits, dead witnesses, and supernatural negotiations.

Gentle Healer - Your reputation is built on saving lives, even when it costs you.
Expertise: Earns trust from civilians, medics, temples, clinics, and wounded NPCs who need reassurance.

Ghost in the System - You are hard to track, hard to identify, and harder to keep out.
Expertise: Helps with digital intrusion, false identities, hidden travel, and avoiding surveillance.

Guild Darling - The Guild likes you, or at least people think it does.
Expertise: Helps with internal favors, softer debriefs, extra briefings, and being trusted by Guild staff.

Guild Problem - The Guild knows your name for all the wrong reasons.
Expertise: Makes officials cautious, draws Reeves attention, and helps when intimidation matters more than approval.

Haunted Eyes - People believe you have seen something no one should see.
Expertise: Draws occultists, trauma survivors, prophets, and NPCs who recognize supernatural scars.

Last Witness - You survived an event everyone else wants forgotten.
Expertise: Draws investigators, enemies, historians, and survivors who need testimony, confirmation, or the truth no official record contains.

Liberator - You have a reputation for freeing prisoners, slaves, hostages, or oppressed communities.
Expertise: Draws rebels, refugees, resistance cells, and people begging for rescue.

Lone Wolf - People believe you work best alone and do not need help.
Expertise: Helps with solo infiltration, quiet intimidation, and NPCs who admire independence.

Lucky Beyond Reason - People believe fate bends around you at the last possible second.
Expertise: Draws gamblers, risk-takers, desperate clients, and people willing to bet on your survival.

Masked Legend - People know the symbol, mask, armor, or calling card before they know your real name.
Expertise: Helps with dramatic entrances, frightening criminals, inspiring civilians, and hiding your identity behind a larger-than-life persona.

Monster Slayer - You are known for killing something most people run from.
Expertise: Attracts frightened towns, hunters, monster experts, and patrons with dangerous creature problems.

MPS Enemy - The Multiverse Preservation Society has reason to hate you.
Expertise: Attracts anti-MPS allies, alerts you to Preservation Society activity, and marks you as a threat to timeline manipulators.

No One's Fool - People know you are hard to trick.
Expertise: Helps with detecting scams, reading suspicious NPCs, resisting manipulation, and spotting bad deals.

Noble Blood - Your name carries the weight of a house, dynasty, clan, or famous lineage.
Expertise: Helps with formal introductions, old debts, court etiquette, and noble negotiations.

Oathbound - People believe you keep promises no matter the cost.
Expertise: Builds trust quickly with honorable factions, frightened clients, and NPCs looking for a reliable agent.

People's Hero - Ordinary people tell stories about you.
Expertise: Helps with shelter, food, local warnings, hidden routes, and communities willing to take risks for you.

Relic Finder - You have a knack for locating things history tried to bury.
Expertise: Helps with treasure leads, museum contacts, artifact rumors, and ruins nobody wants to enter.

Shield of the Weak - People believe you protect children, civilians, and anyone who cannot fight back.
Expertise: Earns trust from communities, families, healers, and NPCs who need protection more than victory.

Silver Tongue - You are known for talking your way into, out of, and around trouble.
Expertise: Helps with negotiations, lies, bargaining, introductions, and fast talk under pressure.

Soft Touch - People believe you can be moved by a sad story.
Expertise: Makes desperate NPCs approach you first, which can reveal hooks, lies, and hidden victims.

Street-Born Survivor - Everyone knows you came up rough and learned to endure.
Expertise: Earns respect from gangs, refugees, laborers, and people who distrust polished authority.

Team Anchor - You are known as the one who keeps the crew together.
Expertise: Helps with morale, mediation, rallying allies, and convincing scared NPCs to cooperate.

Tech Whisperer - Machines seem to make sense to you, even when they should not.
Expertise: Helps with repairs, diagnostics, strange devices, jury-rigging, and conversations with technical crews.

Timeline Scarred - The Multiverse has visibly changed you.
Expertise: Helps with anomaly recognition, strange sympathy from other altered beings, and warnings about timeline danger.

Unkillable - People are convinced you should already be dead.
Expertise: Intimidates enemies, reassures allies, and attracts people who need someone to survive the unsurvivable.

War Veteran - You survived a real war and people can see it in how you move.
Expertise: Helps with soldiers, mercenaries, military logistics, battlefield rumors, and veteran communities.
