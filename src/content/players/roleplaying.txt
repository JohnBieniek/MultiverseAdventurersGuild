At the table, the players and GM share the story, but they have different jobs.

Players:
Each player creates one character: a hero, scoundrel, wizard, cyborg, or anything else that fits the adventure.
You decide what your character says and does.
You role-play their personality, goals, choices, and reactions.

The GM:
Describes the world, locations, people, and dangers.
Plays all non-player characters (NPCs).
Decides when dice rolls are needed and describes what happens as a result.

You usually choose:
Concept - Who are you? Gunslinger, knight, hacker, alien, or something stranger.
Stats - Strength, Dexterity, Education, and the other core traits.
Skills - Sneak, Attack, Influence
Talents - Silver Tongue, Ferocious Attack, Aim
Gear - Weapons, armor, tools.
Your character sheet is your reference during play.
[character-sheet]

Most RPGs follow this rhythm:
GM describes the situation: You enter a dusty frontier town at sunset.
Heroes say what they do: I scan the street for trouble. I head into the saloon.
Dice decide uncertain outcomes: Roll d20 + skill + stat = Basic Roll.
GM narrates the result: You spot armed men watching the door.
Repeat: This loop continues for exploration, social scenes, and combat.

High roll = success, low roll = failure or complication.

Dice add chance and tension.
Rules keep things fair and consistent.
You don't need to memorize everything - the GM helps.
The Guild uses d4, d6, d8, d10, d20
Think of rules as guidelines, not restrictions on creativity.

Combat

Combat is usually more structured. Everyone takes turns.

On your turn, you can move, attack, use abilities, or help allies.

Dice determine hits, damage, and special effects.

The goal isn't just to win fights: it's to make the story exciting.

Role-Playing

You can role-play as much or as little as you like.

In character: I don't trust this deal.

Out of character: My character is suspicious.

There's no wrong way: comfort level matters.

Character Growth

Over time, characters usually gain new options and a changing place in the world.

Experience Points: XP are gained at a rate of 3 per session. Every 10 XP leads to a new level and further benefits.

New Talents: You gain new Talents at certain levels.

Reputation in the world

Story Changes: Your character changes because of the story.

Table Goal

Have fun telling a story together.

You're not competing against the GM or other Heroes.

You're collaborating to create memorable moments.
