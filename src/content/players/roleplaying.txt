Guild Intake Briefing

Before we begin, understand this clearly: you are not here because of where you're from, when you're from, or what world you call home. You're here because you showed potential.

The Guild doesn't care if you're a knight, a hacker, a witch, a gunslinger, or something no one here has a name for yet. If you can survive, adapt, and make decisions under pressure, we can work with you. Our systems will let you build what you already are close enough that it won't matter in the field.

We exist to monitor timelines in flux. When worlds drift, fracture, or start breaking their own rules, we step in. Sometimes that means investigation. Sometimes it means diplomacy. Sometimes it means violence. You'll decide which.

For every successful assignment, compensation is placed into a private account under your name. You can leave the Guild at any time. When you do, the money goes with you. No debt. No ownership. No chains.

You will be sent into a timeline with what you can carry. The Guild cannot provide local currency for every possible reality. If you need money or gear, find it yourself, but do not alter the timeline more than the mission requires.

So. Welcome to the Multiverse Adventurers Guild. Your first job is waiting. Try not to break anything important.

At the table, the players and GM share the story, but they have different jobs.

Players:
Each player creates one character: a hero, scoundrel, wizard, cyborg, or anything else that fits the adventure.
You decide what your character says and does.
You role-play their personality, goals, choices, and reactions.

The GM:
Describes the world, locations, people, and dangers.
Plays all non-player characters (NPCs).
Decides when dice rolls are needed and describes what happens as a result.

You usually choose:
Concept - Who are you? Gunslinger, knight, hacker, alien, or something stranger.
Stats - Strength, Dexterity, Education, and the other core traits.
Skills - Sneak, Attack, Influence
Talents - Silver Tongue, Ferocious Attack, Aim
Gear - Weapons, armor, tools.
Your character sheet is your reference during play.

Most RPGs follow this rhythm:
GM describes the situation: You enter a dusty frontier town at sunset.
Heroes say what they do: I scan the street for trouble. I head into the saloon.
Dice decide uncertain outcomes: Roll d20 + skill + stat = Basic Roll.
GM narrates the result: You spot armed men watching the door.
Repeat: This loop continues for exploration, social scenes, and combat.

High roll = success, low roll = failure or complication.

Dice add chance and tension.
Rules keep things fair and consistent.
You don't need to memorize everything - the GM helps.
The Guild uses d4, d6, d8, d10, d20
Think of rules as guidelines, not restrictions on creativity.

Combat

Combat is usually more structured. Everyone takes turns.

On your turn, you can move, attack, use abilities, or help allies.

Dice determine hits, damage, and special effects.

The goal isn't just to win fights: it's to make the story exciting.

Role-Playing

You can role-play as much or as little as you like.

In character: I don't trust this deal.

Out of character: My character is suspicious.

There's no wrong way: comfort level matters.

Character Growth

Over time, characters usually gain new options and a changing place in the world.

Experience Points: XP are gained at a rate of 3 per session. Track total XP earned and unspent XP separately. Every 10 total XP earned grants a new Level and its benefits; spending unspent XP on improvements does not delay leveling.

New Talents: You gain new Talents at certain levels.

Reputation in the world

Reputation is how people start to recognize you before you ever enter the room. Maybe you become known as a reliable problem-solver, a dangerous duelist, a strange miracle worker, or the person who walked away from something impossible. Reputation can open doors, create complications, attract enemies, or make strangers ask for help.

Story Changes: Your character should not stay frozen in place. Missions leave marks. You might gain allies, make enemies, change your ideals, pick up scars, earn titles, lose trust, or discover a new reason to keep going. Let the events of play reshape who your Hero is becoming.

Table Goal

Have fun telling a story together.

You're not competing against the GM or other Heroes.

You're collaborating to create memorable moments.
