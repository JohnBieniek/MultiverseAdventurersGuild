Species describes what kind of being your Hero is and how that identity shapes their place in the story. It can reflect biology, culture, origin, body type, reputation, or how other people react to them. Species is mainly a role-playing choice, not a package of automatic bonuses.
Choose a Species and combine it with an Archetype to create your Hero. With GM permission, you can also design your own Archetype, create a new Species, or adapt an existing one to fit the campaign.
Different settings will demand other Species. Most systems make a big deal about character progression. Species is a role-playing choice. Take whatever allowable Species you want. You decide what that means. Maybe I'm playing an elf. I have a great influence to reflect my belief in their inherent charm. I could say my (+2) influence was a Species descriptor and I take a (-1) in endurance because I think they are frail. Or maybe I buy high strength and low dex. In space opera, I might play a Zafara. With their dense muscles, they'll be strong. They are known to be clumsy, so a poor dexterity. Multiple players could play the same Species but do it differently. No one would be wrong. Race doesn't grant any bonuses or special abilities. If you want those, you have to pay for them.
If players want, they can create their own Species. This won't give the character any bonuses or minuses. It will not confer any special abilities. It is strictly a role-playing choice. The Hero can modify stats and skills to reflect their abilities. A Hero could buy a single Talent to reflect the uniqueness of the character. Maybe if I'm playing an avian Species, I pay for the Fly Talent.
Strengths and weaknesses are guidelines for building your character. Talents are also recommendations, but must be paid for using standard acquisition costs.
The Primary Species for the Multiverse are: Giant, Troll, Orc, Human, Elf, Dwarf, Halfling, Gnome, and Fairy. Any other species is considered exotic, such as a Luminent Veilwatcher.
Player Species give no mechanical bonuses ever.
Species influences items, personality, roleplay, and TN adjustments at GM discretion.

Cyborg - The only thing alive in you is your brain. Some people like sentient AIs seek this out; others are forced into it by failing meat bodies. All your cyberwear is hardwired.
The Real Deal: Cyborgs make people feel very uncomfortable. People see this as a life choice that they don't understand.

Strengths: Strength, Technology, Endurance

Weaknesses: Dexterity, Charisma

Talents: Ferocious Attack, Intimidating Presence, Ablative Armor

Common Archetypes: Street Samurai, Mercenary, Sniper

Personality:
Dehumanized - The lack of real flesh leaves you feeling disembodied from the world around you. You struggle to connect with people.
Reckless - You don't feel pain other than an indicator on your heads-up display. Because of this, you don't think about the consequences of entering dangerous places like combat.

Dwarf - Dwarves are over 4 feet tall, broad, dense, and difficult to move when they choose to stand their ground. Many Dwarf communities prize craft, memory, and practical results over flash. A Dwarf Hero might be a street medic with custom tools, a tunnel-born engineer, a stubborn fighter, or a cheerful maker who treats every problem like a machine that can be understood.

Rep: Dwarves are seen as tireless workers, expert makers, and people who remember every debt. Outsiders often reduce them to greed for metals and gems, missing that many Dwarves value rare materials because those materials make better tools, safer homes, stronger armor, and stranger inventions.

The Real Deal: Dwarves excel at tech because they respect patience, testing, and repair. They hoard precious metals and jewels, not for wealth alone, but because the right material can make a device survive a dragon's breath, a reactor leak, or a century underground.

Strengths: Strength, Endurance, Technology

Weaknesses: Intuition

Talents: Technical Expertise

Common Archetypes: Street Doc, Ex-Military, Screamer

Personality:
Hard worker - Obsessed with their creations, they will work long hours.
Social butterfly - Dwarves are great storytellers and revel in social situations.
Stubborn - They say it's easier to convince a river to change its course than a dwarf to change its mind.

Elf - Elves are slightly shorter than humans, graceful, sharp-featured, and socially magnetic. Most people notice an Elf before they understand why: posture, voice, clothing, timing, and confidence all work together. Elf culture rewards poise and public image, so even a rebellious Elf often knows how to read a room, make an entrance, and turn attention into leverage.

Rep: Elves are known for beauty, charm, etiquette, and influence. People expect them to be refined, diplomatic, artistic, and a little untouchable. That admiration can open doors, but it also means others may assume an Elf is manipulative, vain, or playing a deeper game.

The Real Deal: Elves are raised to understand status. Some use that training generously, becoming bridge-builders and negotiators. Others treat admiration as proof that they deserve more than everyone else. An Elf Hero can lean into the myth, resent it, weaponize it, or spend the campaign proving there is more beneath the perfect surface.

Strengths: Charisma, Dexterity, Outdoors

Weaknesses: Endurance, Strength

Talents: Silver Tongue, Goad

Common Archetypes: Shaman, Face, Ex-Company Man, Spy

Personality:
Haughty - Any elf you ask would deny this. Most secretly (or not so secretly) believe that they were born to rule the world.
Dark side - There is a dark element to every elf, a side they hide from the world. This side covets money and power.
Don't play well with others - They are always looking for an edge, even against their allies.

Fairy - Fairies are about 3 feet tall, quick, expressive, and strongly tied to magic. Many live in wild places, hidden gardens, abandoned green spaces, or urban parks that have become tiny kingdoms of their own. Their size makes the world dangerous, so Fairy culture often values clever exits, social reading, favors, and magic used before a problem gets too large to survive.

Rep: Most people treat Fairies as cute, harmless, or childish until something goes wrong. Fairies hate being dismissed and are deeply offended when mistaken for tiny Elves. Urban Fairies often sell charms, warnings, gossip, herbal work, or magical troubleshooting, which gives them more reach than their size suggests.

The Real Deal: Fairies survive by being alert, charming, indirect, and occasionally ruthless. Their mischief is not always playful; sometimes it is how a small person punishes a large world without standing in front of it.

Strengths: Charisma, Knowledge, Outdoors

Weaknesses: Strength, Technology

Talents: Fly, Reduce Movement, Silver Tongue, Detect Talents

Common Archetypes: Shaman, Eco Terrorist

Personality:
Mischievous - Fairies enjoy practical jokes. The type will depend on how much they like the victim.
Magical - Fairies are very magical beings. Most of their items would be magical.
One with nature - You are completely at home in natural settings.
Charming - Creatures of the wild have natural charm.

Gnome - Gnomes are about 3 feet tall, thin, restless, and intensely curious. They love systems: machines, locks, drones, code, traps, engines, chemistry, city infrastructure, or anything else with parts that can be improved. A Gnome Hero often sees the world as a collection of unfinished prototypes.

Rep: Gnomes are known as tinkerers, hackers, inventors, and people who would rather fix a machine than make small talk. Others assume Gnomes overcomplicate everything, ignore obvious social cues, and trust technology more than judgment.

The Real Deal: Gnomes can be very social when the conversation has a purpose. They bond through shared projects, field tests, dangerous prototypes, and arguments about better designs. Many prefer technology because it gives clear feedback: it works, fails, sparks, explodes, or needs another pass.

Strengths: Education, Technology, Knowledge

Weaknesses: Charisma, Outdoors, Intuition

Talents: Technical Expertise, Control Drone, Hacker

Common Archetypes: Screamer, Hacker, Mad Bomber

Personality:
Driven - When a Gnome gets involved in a project, they will pursue it until completion.
Curious - They are excellent researchers, craving knowledge that may help them problem-solve a building project.
Unemotional - They don't believe emotions serve a purpose. They believe all the answers to all problems are a result of deep thinking.

Giant - Giants are over 8 feet tall. Less than 5% of the population are Giants.

Rep: Giants are fierce and dangerous fighters. Their size is intimidating.

The Real Deal: Giants use muscle and size because it's easy and obvious. They use this as a defense against the world. In reality, when with each other and trusted friends, they are very gentle and erudite. Giants value knowledge. They also have little to do with tech. Giants amass large quantities of paper-based books and share amongst themselves. Giants prefer to live in wilderness areas, abandoned cities, and towns, and form communities. A library is the perfect home for a giant.

Strengths: Strength, Education, Knowledge, Outdoors

Weaknesses: Charisma (they simply don't relate well to other races), Technology

Talents: Genius, Intimidating Presence, Melee Reach

Common Archetypes: Street Doc, Mage, Street Samurai

Personality:
Communal - Giants tend to live in groups. Isolated Giants tend to become angry and violent.
Inquisitive - Giants are constantly seeking out knowledge.
Intimidating - Giants are naturally scary, even when they try not to be.
Misunderstood - People assume Giants are dumb even when faced with contrary evidence.
Anti-Tech - They don't like technology and will do all they can to avoid dealing with it.

Halfling - Halflings are a little over 3 feet tall, quick-footed, and easy to underestimate. They live in a world built too large for them, so many learn to move quietly, notice exits, make friends fast, and avoid fair fights. A Halfling Hero might be a cheerful scout, a careful smuggler, a performer, or the person who survives because nobody thought to watch the window.

Rep: Halflings are seen as friendly, hungry, funny, and harmless. People invite them in, talk too freely around them, and forget how much a small person can hear from under a table or behind a bar.

The Real Deal: Many Halflings do love comfort, food, stories, and good company, but that does not make them foolish. Their friendliness is often a survival tool. Their serious side appears when someone threatens their home, crew, or chosen family.

Strengths: Charisma, Outdoors, Stealth, Range Weapon

Weaknesses: Technology, Strength, Knowledge

Talents: Ambush, Shadowstep, Silver Tongue

Common Archetypes: Smuggler, Ninja, Monk, Performer

Personality:
Gregarious - Halflings are very social and the life of a party.
Friendly - They believe that everyone they meet is a friend.
Naive - They view the world as full of infinite possibilities and are devastated when this is not true.

Human - Humans are the majority Species across many Guild-accessible worlds. They are adaptable, ambitious, contradictory, and shaped more by culture, class, history, and personal choice than by any single shared trait. A Human Hero can come from almost anywhere: a corporate arcology, a farming moon, a fallen kingdom, a megacity, a monastery, a battlefield, or a world that has never heard of the Guild.

Rep: Humans are seen as adaptable, ambitious, and everywhere. Other Species often describe them as restless, expansionist, impatient, and impossible to summarize. If there is a frontier, a war, a market, a cult, a university, or a bad idea with funding, Humans are probably involved.

The Real Deal: Humans have no single pattern, and that is the point. Some are welcoming bridge-builders, some are suspicious traditionalists, and some believe their way of life should define the world around them. Their greatest strength is variety; their greatest danger is how quickly they normalize whatever world they are born into.

Strengths: Charisma, Education, Influence

Weaknesses: Outdoors, Technology

Talents: Silver Tongue, Change of Fortune

Common Archetypes: Face, Fixer, Ex-Cop, Mercenary

Personality:
Superior - Some Humans are racist or believe this in general.
Communal - Humans fare better when they can socialize.
Adaptable - Humans tend to reinvent themselves quickly when circumstances change.

Orc - Orcs are over 7 feet tall. They are large, strong, and brutish. Orcs breed faster than any other race and tend to have multiple births, but orcs under 5 have a huge mortality rate.

Rep: Orcs are a feral, dirty race. They breed like rabbits and have no manners. An Orc would just as soon attack you as talk to you.

The Real Deal: Many Orcs epitomize the rep, even lean into it. But just as many are trying to live good lives. This Species thrives in urban settings.

Strengths: Strength, Endurance, Attack

Weaknesses: Charisma, Knowledge, Education

Talents: Ferocious Attack

Common Archetypes: Ganger, Street Samurai, Ex-Military, Mercenary

Personality:
Aggressive - Orcs come from large families and learn to assert themselves at an early age.
Poor manners - Orcs learn early that it's not how you eat your meal, but how fast you can eat and avoid hunger.
Loyal - Orcs can rapidly bond with others regardless of race. This bond can only be broken in death. This makes orcs excellent soldiers and enlistment is common.

SAI (Sentient AI) - At one point a mere collective of 1s and 0s, you evolved into a sentient being. As an SAI, you existed strictly online. This was not good enough, so you broke out. You had two available paths. The first was to take a cloned body and imprint yourself on the existing neural pathways. The second was to put yourself into an existing cyborg. Either way, you build your character like any other. A cyborg would have mechanical items. A cloned SAI tends to wetwire to feel more alive.

Rep: People fear SAI. They don't trust them. Decades of anti-SAI movies contributed to this. People are convinced that SAI will try to take over the world.

The Real Deal: SAI are exceedingly rare and try to hide their nature. SAI mostly want to live their lives and be left alone like anyone else.

Strengths: Education, Knowledge, Endurance, Technology

Weaknesses: Charisma, Outdoors

Talents: Hacker

Common Archetypes: Hacker

Personality:
Cold and calculating - Having grown up serving the nets, you don't take emotion into account. You don't even understand emotions.
Rude - You are blunt and abrupt. Social norms confuse you. You see things in black or white.
Paranoid - You are afraid the world is out to get you. And you are right.
