Everything in MAG starts with one rule:
Basic Roll (BR)

Roll a d20 and add:

Stat + Skill + Talent or other Modifiers
Compare the total to a Target Number (TN) set by the GM.

If your total meets or beats the TN, you succeed.
Use this process during Session Zero to build your hero in about 15 minutes. Heroes new to role-playing take longer, as it's important to teach them how to play.
Think about the kind of hero you want to play. Any genre, any era, any setting is allowed in the Multiverse - cyborgs, cowgirls, witches, knights, superheroes, detectives, monks, and everything in between.
Pick the Archetype that best matches your concept. Archetypes give your character direction, style, a few suggested Talents, and ideas about your Hero's personality.
You can change your Archetype early in the campaign as you get familiar with the system.
Giant, Troll, Orc, Human, Elf, Dwarf, and Halfling are the basic races. You can create any Species you want. These are known as the Exotics. Fairy is an example of an Exotic
Select a Species that fits your concept or the setting. Species give you flavor and descriptive traits.

If you're unsure, your GM will summarize races available in the current campaign.
Stats measure your physical, mental, and social strengths.
Assign the following values:

+3, +2, +1, 0, 0, -1
Stats (St)
Strength - this represents melee attacks.
Dexterity - this represents making ranged attacks and controlling vehicles.
Endurance - this represents a character's ability to handle physically grueling tasks and affects hit points. It also reflects stamina.Ex. Hit points, staying alive below 0 hit points.
Intuition - this represents knowing things outside your education. This is that gut feeling you get or the ability to take disparate pieces of information and draw a conclusion. Ex. Initiative, solving problems.
Education - this is what you have learned in life. It could be a college education or things learned by experience.
Charisma - this is your ability to use your personality to get people to do your bidding. Ex Fast talk, intimidate, contacts.
For your character, you get to assign the scores (+3, +2,+1,0,0,-1). The assigned number is the score. This is paired with a descriptor (which explains the score ie. (+2) St - vat muscle).

"Pick your most important Stat and write in (+3). Now look at your least important stat. Write in a (-1). Pick your next most important stat. Write in a (+2). Of the three stats left, put a (+1) into the most important stat and (0)s in the remaining stats."
These numbers are your base scores.
Skills work with Stats to show what you are good at. Every Hero has every skill, but bonuses show specialization.

Your defenses protect you from harm:
Defense = 11 + Defense bonus + (Defense rating x 2)
Resilience = Defense bonus + Strength + Dexterity + Endurance
Ego = Defense bonus + Intuition + Education + Charisma
Defense is used when enemies try to hit you. Resilience resists physical danger and body-based effects. Ego resists fear, influence, mental attacks, and willpower-based effects. Athletics remains a normal skill for climbing, jumping, swimming, and similar physical tasks.

Dual Wield:
Melee - Fight with two d6 weapons for d8 damage, using the same special rules as a d8 weapon.
Range - Fight with two d4 weapons for d6 damage, using the same special rules as a d6 weapon.

Silenced - This makes ranged attacks harder to notice, but it does not make the weapon easier to conceal. The damage decreases by one step. A sniper rifle becomes a d8 weapon. A pistol becomes d4-1.
Weapon Smuggler - This Talent improves weapon concealment. Add your Sneak skill to the concealment TN, and activate Weapon Smuggler for an extra +1d4 per affected weapon.
The philosophy behind damage is that Heroes should be rewarded for fighting at Melee ranges because of the increased risk. Melee damage is slightly more serious than ranged damage.

Level
Level increases with every 10 XP. See the following table: Players spend XP when they want, including in the middle of a mission. For every 10XP, you gain a level and certain advantages. You don't pay for those advantages.
HP = the base HP you have at each level before Endurance is applied. Add your Endurance score once for each level you have, including level 0. Slots indicate how many combat Talents you can have active at any given time.
Force Access Rule (Hard Limit)

You cannot activate or purchase Talents of a Force higher than your level allows.

If you improve your Endurance score, the bonus applies retroactively to all levels. For example, at level 3 with a score of (0), my HP is 28. I buy (+1) endurance. The bonus applies to all levels, giving me 32 HP.
You can switch your talents when you reach a new level.
MAG is a blended system. You gain specific advantages as you reach each level, but you can also spend XP whenever you want.
A player earns 3 XP per playing session. These can be used to improve attributes, skills, talents, and energy. The GM can adjust this XP award up or down depending on how fast or slow they want the campaign to progress. (0) level is essentially a Tutorial for beginning players.
