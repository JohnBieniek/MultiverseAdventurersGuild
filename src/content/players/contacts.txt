You begin with 3 + Charisma Contacts.

Contacts are NPCs you know who can help you with information, equipment, favors, introductions, cover stories, and off-the-books services. You may choose some now and fill in others as you play. Contacts can exist anywhere in the Multiverse; if you have a bartender contact, the GM can place a similar bartender in any setting.

Fence - A criminal merchant who moves goods that cannot be sold openly.
Example Name: Rook Vellum
Expertise: Sells stolen items, appraises black-market gear, finds buyers for illegal loot, and knows who is looking for rare merchandise.

Surgeon - A discreet medical expert who treats wounds without asking too many questions.
Example Name: Dr. Selene Orak
Expertise: Performs surgery, removes tracking devices, patches up injured Heroes, and identifies strange biological effects.

Journalist - A professional investigator with sources in public records, politics, and street rumor.
Example Name: Mina Cross
Expertise: Finds leads, verifies rumors, exposes cover-ups, and gets the party access to people who want their story told.

Cop - A law-enforcement contact who understands procedure, warrants, evidence, and local pressure.
Example Name: Detective Jonas Pike
Expertise: Checks criminal records, warns about raids, explains local law, and quietly points Heroes toward active investigations.

Bartender - A social hub who hears every rumor before it becomes public.
Example Name: Tavi Glass
Expertise: Shares local gossip, introduces useful strangers, hides a message, and helps Heroes read the mood of a neighborhood.

Fixer - A dealmaker who connects people, jobs, equipment, and favors.
Example Name: Cass Meridian
Expertise: Arranges meetings, finds temporary work, hires specialists, and negotiates access to restricted goods or locations.

Mechanic - A practical repair expert who keeps vehicles, drones, armor, and field gear alive.
Example Name: Bolt Navarro
Expertise: Repairs vehicles, modifies equipment, identifies mechanical failures, and rigs emergency solutions from spare parts.

Hacker - A digital infiltrator who knows networks, private systems, and data trails.
Example Name: Zero Vale
Expertise: Breaks into systems, erases records, traces signals, spoofs credentials, and finds hidden data on the private net.

Smuggler - A transport specialist who moves people and cargo through places they should not reach.
Example Name: Nix Calder
Expertise: Bypasses checkpoints, hides contraband, arranges illegal passage, and knows routes that do not appear on official maps.

Archivist - A scholar of records, histories, old maps, and timeline fragments.
Example Name: Professor Ilyan Voss
Expertise: Researches history, translates obscure documents, identifies artifacts, and explains why a forgotten detail matters.

Occultist - A specialist in magic, spirits, rituals, curses, and impossible phenomena.
Example Name: Mara Thorne
Expertise: Identifies supernatural threats, interprets omens, finds ritual components, and explains magical consequences.

Quartermaster - A supply contact who can get ordinary gear quickly and unusual gear with enough warning.
Example Name: Gunny Vale
Expertise: Locates weapons, armor, field kits, ammunition, disguises, and mission supplies before a dangerous assignment.
