You start with one Weapon Enhancement for either Melee or Ranged. Weapon categories describe damage, concealment, and common examples. Rename the weapon however you want for the setting: a d8 melee weapon might be a longsword, fire axe, shock baton, or bone club.

Unarmed or Tiny Melee - Small, natural, or improvised close-range attacks.
Category: Melee
Damage: d4
Concealment: Automatic concealment.
Examples: Martial arts, brawling, claws, brass knuckles, sap.

Light Melee Weapon - Small weapons that can be hidden easily and drawn quickly.
Category: Melee
Damage: d6
Concealment: Automatic concealment.
Examples: Weapon under 18 inches, dagger, switchblade, hatchet, pistol whip.

Medium Melee Weapon - Standard one-handed melee weapons with real reach and threat.
Category: Melee
Damage: d8
Concealment: TN 15 Observation check to conceal.
Examples: Weapon under 3 feet, longsword, shortsword, axe, club, machete, baton.

Heavy Melee Weapon - Large weapons that hit hard but are obvious and harder to defend with.
Category: Melee
Damage: d10
Concealment: No concealment.
Special Rules: -2 Defense while using it.
Examples: Staff, halberd, great axe, great sword, maul, heavy polearm.

Holdout Ranged Weapon - Small ranged weapons that are easy to hide but limited in impact.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d4
Concealment: Automatic concealment.
Examples: Revolver, handgun, sling, slingshot, compact blaster.

Compact Ranged Weapon - Short ranged weapons with more punch, but harder to hide.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d6
Concealment: TN 15 Observation check to conceal.
Examples: Submachine gun, Uzi, Ingram, bow, crossbow, compact carbine.

Longarm Ranged Weapon - Full-sized ranged weapons built for serious fights.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d8
Concealment: TN 12 Observation check to conceal.
Examples: Assault rifle, AR-15, AK-47, shotgun, hunting rifle, battle bow.

Heavy Ranged Weapon - Large ranged weapons that need space, bracing, or commitment.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d10
Concealment: No concealment.
Special Rules: Braced.
Examples: Sniper rifle, machine gun, anti-materiel rifle, heavy crossbow.

Two-Weapon Fighting (Melee) - Fight with two light melee weapons.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: Two d6 melee weapons deal d8 damage and use the same special rules as a d8 melee weapon.

Two-Weapon Fighting (Ranged) - Fight with two holdout ranged weapons.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: Two d4 ranged weapons deal d6 damage and use the same special rules as a d6 ranged weapon.

Silencers - Suppressors make ranged weapons easier to hide but reduce damage.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: A silencer improves concealment by one step and reduces damage by one step. A pistol becomes a TN 15 Observation check to conceal and deals d4-1 damage. A sniper rifle becomes a d8 weapon.

Concealment - Hiding a weapon is easier for sneaky Heroes.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: Add your Sneak skill to the concealment TN. If you have Sneak +2, you conceal a compact ranged weapon on TN 17 instead of TN 15.

Damage Philosophy - Melee is dangerous because it puts you close to the enemy.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: Heroes are rewarded for fighting at melee range because of the increased risk. Melee damage is slightly more serious than ranged damage.
