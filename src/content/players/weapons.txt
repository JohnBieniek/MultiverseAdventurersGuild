You start with one Weapon Enhancement for either Melee or Ranged. Weapon categories describe damage, concealment, and common examples. Rename the weapon however you want for the setting: a d8 melee weapon might be a longsword, fire axe, shock baton, or bone club. Heroes are rewarded for fighting at melee range because of the increased risk, so melee damage is slightly more serious than ranged damage.

Unarmed or Tiny Melee - Small, natural, or improvised close-range attacks.
Category: Melee
Damage: d4
Concealment: Automatic concealment.
Examples: Martial arts, brawling, claws, brass knuckles, sap.

Light Melee Weapon - Small weapons that can be hidden easily and drawn quickly.
Category: Melee
Damage: d6
Concealment: Automatic concealment.
Examples: Weapon under 18 inches, dagger, switchblade, hatchet, pistol whip.

Medium Melee Weapon - Standard one-handed melee weapons with real reach and threat.
Category: Melee
Damage: d8
Concealment: TN 15 Observation check to conceal.
Examples: Weapon under 3 feet, longsword, shortsword, axe, club, machete, baton.

Heavy Melee Weapon - Large weapons that hit hard but are obvious and harder to defend with.
Category: Melee
Damage: d10
Concealment: No concealment.
Special Rules: -2 Defense while using it.
Examples: Staff, halberd, great axe, great sword, maul, heavy polearm.

Holdout Ranged Weapon - Small ranged weapons that are easy to hide but limited in impact.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d4
Concealment: Automatic concealment.
Examples: Revolver, handgun, sling, slingshot, compact blaster.

Compact Ranged Weapon - Short ranged weapons with more punch, but harder to hide.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d6
Concealment: TN 15 Observation check to conceal.
Examples: Submachine gun, Uzi, Ingram, bow, crossbow, compact carbine.

Longarm Ranged Weapon - Full-sized ranged weapons built for serious fights.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d8
Concealment: TN 12 Observation check to conceal.
Examples: Assault rifle, AR-15, AK-47, shotgun, hunting rifle, battle bow.

Heavy Ranged Weapon - Large ranged weapons that need space, bracing, or commitment.
Category: Ranged
Damage: d10
Concealment: No concealment.
Special Rules: Braced.
Examples: Sniper rifle, machine gun, anti-materiel rifle, heavy crossbow.

Paired Weapons - Fight with a weapon in each hand.
Category: Rule
Melee: Two d6 melee weapons deal d8 damage and use the same special rules as a d8 melee weapon.
Ranged: Two d4 ranged weapons deal d6 damage and use the same special rules as a d6 ranged weapon.

Suppressors - Make ranged attacks harder to notice, but reduce impact.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: A suppressor does not make a weapon easier to conceal; it adds bulk. It can make it harder for bystanders or guards to notice where a shot came from, at the GM's discretion. Suppressed weapons reduce damage by one step: a pistol deals d4-1 damage, and a sniper rifle becomes a d8 weapon.

Braced - Heavy ranged weapons need setup or support.
Category: Rule
Mechanic: A braced weapon must be fired from a stable position, such as prone, mounted, rested on cover, or supported by a bipod or similar gear. The GM may deny the shot or apply disadvantage when the weapon is fired without bracing.
