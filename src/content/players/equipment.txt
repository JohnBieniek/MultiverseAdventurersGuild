Items explain why your Stats and Skills look the way they do.

They don't change numbers; they describe your character. Most items are equipment, such as a Ring of Protection. Some can be from Species, Archetype.

Examples:
(+2) Strength - Giant Species.
(+1) Education - Street-taught scavenger tech.
(-1) Sneak - Loud, heavy boots
Items help define who you are and where you come from. Think of these as gear for your Hero, except they don't have a price tag on them

Guild Intake Briefing

Before we begin, understand this clearly: you are not here because of where you're from, when you're from, or what world you call home. You're here because you showed potential.

The Guild doesn't care if you're a knight, a hacker, a witch, a gunslinger, or something no one here has a name for yet. If you can survive, adapt, and make decisions under pressure, we can work with you. Our systems will let you build what you already are close enough that it won't matter in the field.

We exist to monitor timelines in flux. When worlds drift, fracture, or start breaking their own rules, we step in. Sometimes that means investigation. Sometimes it means diplomacy. Sometimes it means violence. You'll decide which.

For every successful assignment, compensation is placed into a private account under your name. You can leave the Guild at any time. When you do, the money goes with you. No debt. No ownership. No chains.

You will be sent into a timeline with what you can carry. The Guild cannot provide local currency for every possible reality. If you need money or gear, find it yourself, but do not alter the timeline more than the mission requires.

So. Welcome to the Multiverse Adventurers Guild. Your first job is waiting. Try not to break anything important.

Vision Pack

The Vision Pack is spectral augmentation optics issued or approved by the Guild. It lets Heroes interpret unfamiliar battlefields, strange lighting, hidden heat signatures, and distant threats without needing a different tool for every timeline.

Visual Modes: Low-light vision, thermal/infrared vision, and magnification zoom up to 20x.

Switching Modes: Switching modes is a Free Action.

Forms: The Vision Pack can appear as a cybernetic eye implant, contact-lens mesh, goggles, or a helmet visor.

SAFE Weapon System

Modern Guild-compatible weapons often include S.A.F.E. systems: Specialized Accuracy Friend/Foe Exclusion Systems. The weapon interfaces with the user's nervous system through the hand, usually through gloves, allowing direct neural input.

Friend/Foe Safety: The user labels combatants as friend, non-combatant, or foe. The weapon prevents rounds from being fired downrange at a friendly target.

Thought Trigger: SAFE weapons do not need traditional triggers. They are fired by thought through the neural interface.

Authorized User: SAFE weapons are locked to an authorized user through genetic patterns. You cannot be shot with your own gun.

Security: SAFE systems are considered unhackable because the direct neural input is not externally networked.

Team Initiative: SAFE systems aid coordinated attacks. Every Hero rolls Initiative, but the Heroes may all act on the highest Hero initiative roll. A Hero can still choose a different Initiative, such as waiting for an enemy action, and may spend a Reaction to act at that moment.

Timeline Conformity

Guild weapons automatically conform to the visible technology of the timeline. If a player brings a plasma gun into a low-tech setting, the Multiverse makes it appear appropriate to that universe. The weapon still operates normally.
